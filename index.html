Kosovo DPM
Monitoring Platform

Dog Population Management (DPM) is the coordinated, humane, and evidence-based approach to reducing the number of street dogs — through sterilisation, vaccination, registration, and responsible ownership. This platform centralises Kosovo's first nationwide street dog population data, collected across all 38 municipalities between June 2024 and May 2025. It provides institutions, municipalities, and civil society with a transparent, data-driven baseline to plan interventions, track progress, and allocate resources effectively.

📍
Survey Design & Sampling Strategy

A standardised direct observation method was employed across all 38 municipalities, adapted from Hiby & Hiby (2017). In each municipality, a minimum of four survey routes were selected to representatively sample urban, semi-urban, and rural areas. Known hotspots such as landfills were intentionally excluded to avoid overestimation and bias.

Survey routes totalled 20–150 km per municipality and were conducted mainly on residential and secondary roads, where street dogs are most likely to be encountered. Data was collected digitally using the Talea mobile application, which records GPS tracks, timestamps, and structured per-dog observation fields. Talea functions both online and offline, with data stored in a secure cloud database.

Survey timing: June 2024 (Podujeva), October 2024 (Lipjan, Obiliq, Fushë Kosovë, Graçanica), and April–May 2025 (all remaining municipalities).

📐
Population Estimation

Street network data were extracted from OpenStreetMap and filtered to include only residential and secondary roads, aligning with the road types surveyed during fieldwork. Detection probabilities were generated using the SuperDuplicates tool (Tiwari et al., 2018), applying Good-Turing frequency theory to account for unseen individuals.

Dogs per km = Total Dog Sightings ÷ Total Survey Route Length (km)

Estimated Dog Population = Dogs per km × Total Street Length (km) ÷ Detectability

A default detectability of 0.35 was applied to all municipalities based on photo-mark–recapture studies in rural and semi-urban areas. In municipalities where individual photo-mark–recapture studies were conducted, individual detectability results were used (e.g. Prishtina: 0.58). All surveys were conducted during early morning hours around sunrise under optimal weather conditions (clear or overcast, no rain, fog or strong winds).

📊
Data Collected & Sterilisation Assessment

For each dog observed, the following data were recorded: sex, sterilisation status (based on ear-tag presence), age category (puppy/adult), lactating female status, visible health indicators (injury, skin condition, 5-scale body condition score), sign of ownership (collar), GPS location, and photographic documentation for mark-resight analysis.

Each municipality was surveyed over two consecutive mornings. In selected municipalities, a photo-mark–recapture approach was implemented over three consecutive mornings to derive individual detectability coefficients.

Sterilisation % is calculated as the proportion of sterilised adult dogs among those with known sterilisation status. Dogs classified as puppies (under 4 months) and those with indeterminate sterilisation status are excluded from this calculation. The national and regional sterilisation rates aggregate all observations across municipalities using the same exclusion criteria.

All field teams underwent standardised training prior to deployment. Collected data were subject to cross-validation procedures to verify accuracy and completeness.

Legal Basis Kosovo legislation governing animal welfare and dog population management
Primary Legislation
Law No. 02/L-10 on Animal Welfare
Kosovo Assembly · 2006

Primary legislative framework governing animal keeping, care, shelter, breeding, and transport in Kosovo. Establishes the responsibilities of MAFRD and the Food and Veterinary Agency (FVA), and sets administrative sanctions for violations (€100–5,000).

Primary Legislation
Law No. 2004/21 — The Veterinary Law
Kosovo Assembly · 2004

Establishes the legal framework for veterinary services in Kosovo, including the mandate of the Food and Veterinary Agency (FVA). Provides the institutional basis for state-funded sterilisation programmes and veterinarian engagement in dog population management.

View in Official Gazette →
National Strategy
National Strategy for Dog Population Management 2023–2028
Ministry of Local Government Administration · 2022

Kosovo's official strategic framework for humane, effective, and sustainable dog population management, based on EU best practices. Key measures include CNVR programmes, mandatory registration of owned dogs, and coordinated inter-institutional data collection.

View National Strategy →
Secondary Legislation
Administrative Instruction (MAFRD) No. 02/2023 — Animal Pet Shelters
Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Rural Development · 2023

Defines minimum technical standards for animal pet shelters in Kosovo, covering facility infrastructure, animal housing, veterinary care, and licensing procedures. Recognises CNVR as an optional tool for managing street dog populations.

View in Official Gazette →
Secondary Legislation
Administrative Instruction (MAFRD) No. 27/2024 — Pet Animal Breeders
Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Rural Development · 2024

Sets technical requirements for pet animal breeders in Kosovo, covering keeping, donation, and sale conditions. Bans the sale of dogs and cats in pet shops and introduces mandatory breeding licences, addressing unregulated breeding as a driver of the street dog population.

View in Official Gazette →
Secondary Legislation
Administrative Instruction (MAFRD) No. 02/2018 & 02/2021 — Pet Animal Registration
Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Rural Development · 2018, amended 2021

Establishes mandatory microchipping and registration of pet animals in Kosovo. The 2021 amendment made abandonment officially forbidden. Effective enforcement is a prerequisite for reducing abandonment as a primary driver of the street dog population.

About this Platform Organisations and individuals behind this initiative
Survey Implementation · Platform Co-Owner
Animal Rights Foundation (ARF) Kosovo

ARF Kosovo is the leading animal welfare organisation in Kosovo. ARF implemented the nationwide street dog survey, manages the data collection methodology, and co-owns and maintains this platform. ARF has been monitoring dog population management projects in Kosovo since 2008.

animalrights-rks.org →
Strategic Partner · Survey Funder
FOUR PAWS International

FOUR PAWS International is a global animal welfare organisation that strategically designed and financed Kosovo's first nationwide street dog population survey. FOUR PAWS advocates for proactive, long-term, humane solutions to street dog management and works in Kosovo on CNVR programmes in multiple municipalities.

four-paws.org →
Platform Developer
Arif Aliu

This platform was conceived and developed by Arif Aliu as part of ARF Kosovo's work to make dog population management data publicly accessible and useful for institutional decision-making. The platform integrates survey data, calculation methodology, and public project tracking into a single transparency tool for Kosovo's institutions, NGOs, and the public.
